Types of PTE Tests

There are two types of PTE tests: PTE Academic Test and PTE General Test. Both types of PTE tests have four sub-tests – Reading, Writing, Listening and Speaking to determine one’s English language skills.

PTE Academic test is explicitly designed for students who wish to study in English-speaking countries. PTE scores work as proof of English proficiency for universities to ensure the student can understand and access the study materials given in English. Many countries including Australia and New Zealand specifically refer to the PTE test for admission. PTE Academic test results are only valid for two years from the release of the result.

PTE General test is specially designed for those who are planning to go abroad for work or migration. While PTE academic test is taken in four parts (Reading, Writing, Listening and Speaking), the PTE General test has two main sections – Writing and Speaking. The Writing part in the PTE General exam further splits into Listening, Reading, and Writing sections. Unlike PTE Academic results, PTE General test results do not have an expiry date.

Speaking and Writing Tests

Your speaking and writing test is taken in a single section to check your English speaking and writing skills The writing test is conducted first and then the speaking test. This section focuses on grammar and the time allocated for the speaking and writing test is for 54-67 minutes. This section comprises six small sections and seven different question types. This task consists of:

  • Personal
  • IntroductionRead aloud
  • Repeat sentence
  • Describe image
  • Re-tell lecture
  • Answer short question
  • Summarize written text
  • Essay

    • Reading Test

    Reading test in PTE exam is designed to evaluate the basis of their ability to understand the written instructions in English. Examinee are given 32 to 41 minutes of time for solving questions of reading test. Reading section contains five different question types which are:

  • Multiple Fill in the Blanks
  • Multiple choice questions, multiple answers
  • Re-order paragraphs
  • Fill in the blanks
  • Multiple choice questions, single answer

    • Listening Test

    The Listening test in the PTE exam is designed to determine one’s ability to understand spoken English. For this part of the test, candidates are allocated 30 to 43 minutes within which they have to carefully listen to the audio file played, retain what they heard and answer the questions accordingly. Each audio played usually are 60-90 seconds long. The Listening test contain eight different question types which are:

  • Summarize spoken text
  • Multiple choice questions, multiple answers
  • Fill the blanks
  • Highlight the correct summary
  • Multiple choice questions, single answer
  • Select missing word
  • Highlight incorrect words
  • Write from dictation
